## Task 1: Business Understanding

## Overview

This project was completed as part of the **BCG X Data Science Job Simulation** on **Forage**.

The objective was to understand the client's business problem before touching any data — framing PowerCo's customer churn issue through a data science lens, and drafting an email outlining the data needed and the techniques to be used to investigate it.

---

## Business Scenario

**PowerCo** is a major gas and electricity utility supplying small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). With more competition in the energy market than ever before, PowerCo is concerned about customer **churn** — customers leaving for other providers.

The Associate Director's working hypothesis was that customers have become more **price sensitive** and are switching to lower-cost providers. Before jumping into the data, my task was to work through the problem from a business standpoint and outline a plan of attack:

1. What are all the possible reasons a customer might stay with or switch energy providers (price, customer service, contract terms, energy source, etc.)?
2. What data would be needed to test whether price sensitivity specifically is driving churn?
3. What techniques would be used to analyze that data and validate (or disprove) the hypothesis?

---

## Tools Used

- Business framing / structured problem-solving
- Written business communication (email)
- BCG's 5-step data science methodology

---

## Approach

### 1. Understand the Client and the Problem

- Reviewed the brief from PowerCo and the context provided by the Lead Data Scientist, Estelle Altazin.
- Identified that the core business question was whether **price sensitivity** is a significant driver of customer churn, as opposed to other possible factors (service quality, contract type, competitor offers, etc.).

### 2. Identify Data Requirements

Before analysis can begin, the right data needs to be requested from the client. I identified the key data points needed to investigate the churn/price sensitivity hypothesis, including:

- Historical customer data (consumption, contract dates, sales channel, number of products, etc.)
- Historical pricing data (variable and fixed pricing at different points in time, across peak/off-peak/mid-peak periods)
- Churn indicator (whether each customer churned)

### 3. Outline the Analytical Approach

Mapped out the techniques that would be used to investigate the hypothesis, following BCG X's broader methodology:

1. **Business understanding & problem framing** – why are we solving this problem?
2. **Exploratory data analysis & data cleaning** – what does the data look like?
3. **Feature engineering** – can the dataset be enriched to better capture price sensitivity?
4. **Modeling & evaluation** – can churn be predicted reliably from this data?
5. **Insights & recommendations** – how do we communicate this to the business?

### 4. Draft the Email

Summarized the above into a clear, concise email to the Lead Data Scientist outlining the data required from the client and the proposed approach to investigate the churn problem.
